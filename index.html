Tunku Abdul Rahman Putra Al-Haj

The man who fought for Malaysia

Tunku Abdul Rahman Putra Al-Haj

Interesting facts:

  • The first Prime Minister of Malaysia.
  • Referred to as the Father of Independence of Malaysia thanks to his efforts in freeing Malaysia from British.
  • The seventh son out of 45 children of Sultan Abdul Hamid Halim Shah, the twenty-sixth ruler of Kedah.
  • Graduated from St. Catharine's College in Bachelor Degree of Arts in Law and History.
  • Worked as district officer when he returned to Tanah Melayu.
  • Became President of the Session Court after he finished his law studies after World War 2.
  • Became President of United National Organization (UMNO) in 1951 and worked with Malayan Chinese Association (MCA) to form Alliance Party, then joined by Malaysian Indian Congress (MIC).
  • Became Chief Minister after winning 1955 Malayan general election.
  • Held constitutional talk with Lennox-Boyd in 1956 to discuss Malayan's independence and finally signed Independence agreement for August 1957.
  • Served as the foreign Minister and the prime minister of Malaya at the same time.
  • Resigned as prime minister in 1970 and UMNO president in 1971
  • Died on 6 December 1990 at the age of 87.
